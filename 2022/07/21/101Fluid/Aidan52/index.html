Example - Heat Conductio in a Bar

  • heat conduction in a 1D bar between two fixed temperatures

  • conservation of energy for cell 2
  • for all cells, in matrix form $\boldsymbol{AT=B}$
    • the lengh of the $T$ vector = number of cells in the mesh
    • every row in the matrix equation represents conservation of energy for a particular cell in the mesh

  • between the centroids, the temperature profile is linear

  • $Q$ heat flux across each face

Energy Imbalance

  • CFD uses an iterative approach

Residual

  • (raw) residual represents the local energy imbalance in that particular cell
  • the (modified) energy balance equation $\boldsymbol{AT-B=r}$
    • assess how accurate the solution is at the end of each iteration
    • substitue the current solution $\boldsymbol{T}$ and calculate $\boldsymbol{r}$
    • the closer $\boldsymbol{r}$ is to zero, the more accurate the current solution
  • discrete form:

Momentum equation

  • the residual represents the force imbanlance in the $x$ direction
  • each transport equation has its own residual, in each cell

Geneal form

  • $\boldsymbol{\Phi}$ reprsents the independent variable in any of the transport equations solved ($T,U,V,W,k,\epsilon$)

Accessing convergence

  • the residual vector is a scalar field (like temperature / pressure)

Representative residual

  • a single value that is representative of the residual in all the cells

  • this representative residual is calculated diffrently by different CFD codes

  • The $L_1$ norm

  • The $L_2$ norm
  • The $L$ norm

Scaling

The need for scaling

Scaled residual

  • $n$ is the scaling factor
  • It is representative of the heat flux or momentum flux through each of the cells in the mesh
  • $A_p$ diagonal coefficient
  • The reduction in the orders of magnitude of the scaled residual are used to asees convergence, not the exact value.

Note The excat value of $r_s$ should not be relied upon. The differences may be caused in scaling factor and not mecessarily more / less converged.
