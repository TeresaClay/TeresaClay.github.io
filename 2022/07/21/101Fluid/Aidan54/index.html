    1D flow in a channel

    • Local mass imbalance (continuity residual)
      the difference between the mass flow rate out of the left face of the cell and the right face of the cell

    • Continuity residual
      every cell in the mesh has its own mas imbalance (residual vector)

    • A representative mass imbalance for all the cells in the mesh
      in the same mammer as the residuals, $L_1,L_2,L_\infty$

    3D cells

    • the mass imbalance is the sum of the mass fluxes out of all of the faces of the cell
    • equivalent to the rate of mass creation inside the cell

    Automatic Scaling

    Recap for energy equation

    Continuity equatin

    • For pressure-based solvers, the continuity equation is not formulated as a matrix equation
    • The solution of the momentum equations ($U,V,W$) are used to calculate the mass imbalance in each cell
    • A pressure-correction equation is then solved, with the aim of reducing the mass imbalance in each cell (SIMPLE)
      • You can think of this as increasing the pressure in each cell to force out the extra mass that the mass imbalance tells us what we currently have ( sehr gute Erklärung )

    Note: As a matrix equation is not assembled and solved, there is not a clear method of obtaining a representative mass flux through the cells. Therefore, rather than scaling the mass imbalance, the mass imbalance is normalised instead.

    Normalisation

    • Normalised (divided by maxmimum value)

    • It is common to use the maximum value from the first 5 iterations for normalization

    • 原因： if the solution diverges, then the maximum mass imbalance keep increasing

    • Scaled (divided by the mass flux through the cell)

    Normalisation Limitations

    • This could erroneously lead to the conclusion that the simulation is not converged

    Summary

    • The saled/normalized residual is representative of the local imbalance in every cell in the mesh
